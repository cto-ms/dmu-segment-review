Philip J. Mango, Ph.D., is a licensed psychotherapist with over 30 years of experience in individual, premarital, and marital therapy. He co-founded and directed the St. Michael's Institute for the Psychological Sciences in New York, and served as a visiting professor at the John Paul II Institute for Studies on Marriage and Family. Dr. Mango's areas of expertise include sexuality and chastity, masculinity, and femininity. He worked closely with Blessed Mother Teresa over a 12-year period, providing psychological counseling for her religious sisters. Dr. Mango holds a Ph.D. from Clayton University. He is married with three children.
