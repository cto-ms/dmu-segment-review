Rev. Richard John Neuhaus (1936–2009) was a preeminent American Catholic intellectual and public theologian who founded the Institute on Religion and Public Life and served as editor of First Things magazine. Originally a Lutheran pastor who later converted to Catholicism, Neuhaus was a prophetic voice bringing Christian ethics into civic discourse through his influential writings and public engagement. His prolific contributions to Catholic social teaching, religious freedom, and the pro-life movement established him as one of the most significant Catholic voices in American public policy and religious thought of the late twentieth century.
